Plugin overview

This plugin lets you display appointments from your Google Calendar into your blog.

Setup

  1. Make the cache folder writeable

    If you have administrative access to your webserver, you probably already know how to do this securely. If not, the easy way to do it is to load up your preferred FTP program and connect to your web host's FTP server. Browse to the nucleus/plugins/GoogleCalendar/cache folder and modify the cache folder's properties (or attributes). It should look something like this:

    Click OK and you should be ready to proceed.

  2. Enable public sharing for your Google Calendar

    Note that this step will make your Google Calendar viewable by anyone else on Google Calendar, but if you're willing to show your appointments on your blog then that shouldn't be a problem anyway.

    Log in to Google Calendar and click on Manage Calendars in the little blue Calendars section on the left. Locate the calendar you want to enable sharing for and click the Edit Settings link. Then make sure the Share all information with everyone radio button is selected, like so:

    Click the Save button and let's move on.

  3. Find your calendar's public XML file

    Click on Manage Calendars again and this time click on your calendar's name. This should bring you to your Calendar Details tab. Look near the bottom of the page for the Calendar Address section and find the orange XML button. (Note that you do NOT want the Private Address set of buttons.)

    Right-click this button and select Copy Link Location or your browser's equivalent. It should look something like http://www.google.com/calendar/feeds/your_account@gmail.com/public/basic.

  4. Edit your Nucleus skin

    Log in to your Nucleus admin page and click on Skin Files in your sidebar. Navigate to the file you want to put your Google Calendar in. At the appropriate place, insert this code:
    <%GoogleCalendar(http://www.google.com/calendar/feeds/your_account@gmail.com/public/basic)%>

  5. Customise your calendar's appearance

    Congratulations! At this point your Google Calendar appointments should be showing up on your blog. Chances are, however, that the layout won't match your blog skin's layout.

    Go back to your Nucleus Admin page and click on the Settings link for your blog. (Yes, the settings for this plugin are in your blog settings, not under Plugin options.) Scroll down to the NP_GoogleCalendar section and you will find the various options for the display of your appointments. Modify them to your liking, then click the Change Settings button.

Template Options

In the blog's settings page, you can specify how appointments will appear on your page. You can use these variables to display appointment-specific information in the templates:
  • <%name%>: the event title
  • <%desc%>: a brief description of the event
  • <%date%>: the event's start date
  • <%link%>: the URL for the Google Calendar page for the event
